Appalachia — Virginia

For the ridge, the hollow, and the people between them.

Appalachian Vanguard is a grassroots effort to preserve our region's culture, protect its land, and give our communities a stronger voice in the decisions made about us and for us.

Mission

Who we are

We're a group of people from Southwest Virginia who believe Appalachia's story is too often told by outsiders — and too rarely told well. Appalachian Vanguard exists to change that: to document our history honestly, celebrate our culture without caricature, and organize around the issues that actually shape daily life here.

We're currently an independent, volunteer-run effort. As this work grows, we intend to formalize as a nonprofit organization so we can support the region more directly — through events, advocacy, and resources for the communities we come from.

History & Culture

What we come from

Central Appalachia's story runs through coal camps and company towns, through the mine wars fought over the right to organize, and through generations who built entire communities around an industry now in steep decline. That history left scars — but it also forged a fierce, practical resilience that still defines the region.

It runs through music, too: old-time fiddle and banjo tunes carried over by Scots-Irish and English settlers, reshaped in these hollows into something distinctly ours, and passed down at porch pickin's and church gatherings long before it had a name like "bluegrass." It runs through food — soup beans and cornbread, ramps in spring, canning in the fall — and through a storytelling tradition that treats a good tale as a form of currency.

Land & Place

Stewardship of the mountains, waterways, and working land that generations of our families have called home.

Tradition

Music, craft, dialect, and story — preserved as living practice, not museum pieces.

Community

Public events and mutual aid that bring people together across the towns and counties of the region.

FAQ

Common questions

Is Appalachian Vanguard a registered nonprofit?+
Not yet. We're currently an independent, volunteer-run effort. We intend to pursue formal 501(c)(3) nonprofit registration as the organization grows.
Where does Appalachian Vanguard operate?+
We're based in Southwest Virginia, with a focus on Central Appalachia more broadly.
How can I get involved?+
Reach out through the contact email below. We'll share opportunities to help as public events, volunteer roles, and other ways to plug in take shape.
Why doesn't Appalachian Vanguard name its members or leadership?+
We keep the focus on the region and the work rather than any individual. It also protects the privacy of volunteers who are still building this alongside other jobs and commitments.
Is my email kept private if I contact you?+
Yes. Messages come to a dedicated, private email account and are not shared, sold, or published.
Can I donate?+
Not at this time. We're holding off on accepting donations until nonprofit registration is complete, so we can offer proper tax documentation.
